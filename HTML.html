Welcome to the Learn about Himalayas

The Himalayas are the highest mountain range in the world, stretching across five countries: India, Nepal, Bhutan, China, and Pakistan. Home to Mount Everest, the tallest peak on Earth, the Himalayas are not just a natural wonder but also a spiritual and cultural landmark. The name "Himalaya" means "abode of snow" in Sanskrit, and the region is known for its breathtaking landscapes, glaciers, alpine forests, and sacred rivers like the Ganges. The Himalayas play a vital role in influencing climate, biodiversity, and water resources in South Asia.
